GriefBot is a piece of interactive fiction that explores the emerging concept of AI-powered deathbots. In this new digital age, the lines between what is human and what is digital are increasingly blurry. There are two guiding questions behind this project. Can technology replace the ones we love? More importantly, why would we want it to?

It’s a morbid concept. As we speak, companies are creating deathbots with AI to profit off of grieving people who miss their loved ones. Some people find it fascinating and meaningful, some people find it disgusting and sterile, and most people haven’t heard of it. GriefBot invites you to “play” with the idea. It’s not a perfectly tailored experience, but it’s just jarring enough to be disturbing.

This project was made in Twine. I specifically used the Trialogue format by Philo van Kemenade, which is a chat-style Twine format built on Snowman. I wrote the dialogue directly in the Twine editor, and then exported it as Twee to add the ChatGPT-esque wrapper and the ability to customize bot names.

I hope that, if nothing else, this project makes people stop and think.
